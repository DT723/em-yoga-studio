Yoga

I like teaching people who are skeptical about yoga. We'll move. We'll breathe. Two things you do all the time, every day. It’s simple. I promise. I teach interactive yoga via Zoom. I like to have a dialogue, where clients can ask questions or chime in if they need clarification about something. Zoom allows me to demonstrate a pose and get to see my students in real time, so I can give suggestions, modifications and words of encouragement. Sign up for a free Zoom class.

I found yoga super intimidating for a long time. I took my first yoga class at the 92nd Street Y more than 20 years ago. I pretty much hated it. I didn’t feel bendy or flexible enough. Everyone around me seemed to know what was happening. I didn’t get all the chanting, and the Sanskrit. I would have rather been doing anything else, but I did feel calmer.

After finishing grad school in 2011, I went to Costa Rica for my first yoga retreat, which I didn’t realize was a 100 hour teacher training. My science geek awoke with the anatomy lessons, and the combination of the physical/breathe/mind practice awoke a dormant spiritual interest. When I got back to DC, I completed 500 hours of training through YogaWorks. Curious? Want to know more? Email me here and we can arrange a time to talk.
